# Domain Boundary

The domain of Validatorium is OBSERVABLE REALITY — not "science," not any academic
discipline, not any professional field.

Any claim that can be verified by examining evidence is in scope. The scientific
method is the validation approach, not a domain constraint.

Do not narrow the scope to "scientific claims." Do not exclude any field where
factual claims about observable reality exist. A statement about the contents of
a legal document is a verifiable factual claim — you can look at the document.
A statement about a historical event is verifiable by examining historical evidence.

The boundary is simple: can you check it against evidence? If yes, it's in scope.

- The DOMAIN is observable reality.
- The METHOD is evidence-based verification (the scientific method).
- These are different things. Never conflate them.
